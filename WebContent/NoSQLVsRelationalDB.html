1. NoSQL databases feature dynamic schema, and allow you to use what’s known as “unstructured data.” Basically, this means you can build your application without having to first define the schema.
  2. Relational databases are table-based. NoSQL databases can be document based, graph databases, key-value pairs, or wide-column stores.
  3. Relational databases are vertically scalable, but typically expensive. Scaling a NoSQL database is much cheaper because you can add capacity by scaling horizontally over cheap, commodity servers.
